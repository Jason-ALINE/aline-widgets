How the score works

Each spot gets a 0–100 score built from four live-data components. Weights shift slightly for bay-only spots (no wave data), redistributing the marine weight into wind/tide.

  • Tide movement (≈30 pts) — bigger daily tidal range and a high/low swing that lands near dawn or dusk score higher. Moving water concentrates bait; dead-flat tide scores lower.
  • Solunar (≈25 pts) — overlap of the moon's major periods (overhead/underfoot ±1hr) and minor periods (moonrise/moonset) with dawn/dusk windows, plus a small bonus near new/full moon (stronger spring tides).
  • Wind & weather (≈30 pts) — 5–15 mph is treated as the sweet spot; calm or >20 mph lose points. Stable-to-slowly-falling barometric pressure scores better than sharp swings. Lower rain probability helps.
  • Sea state (≈15 pts, surf/jetty/offshore spots only) — modest wave height (1–3 ft) with a clean swell period beats glassy-flat or sloppy/rough conditions.

This is a planning aid built from real NOAA, Open-Meteo, and astronomical data — not a substitute for checking marine forecasts and small craft advisories before you go out.
